About

As this is a capstone course for software engineering technology programs, student members of Kutoro Games worked collaboratively to design and implement a game as the project product.

Thankfully given permission and advised by Professor Hao Lac, all project documentation will be reflected in the GitHub repository of Space Chase. A midterm and final presentation will be heald to demonstrate the game.

Game Development Background

Race against the evil hunters right under your fingertips as a Spirit Agent! Reach the warp gate to seal your win. There are no limitations to your control, as you can twist and turn in all directions - up, down, left, and right. But wait, there’s more! Immerse yourself in the the chase with gorgeous low polygon animated 3D models to make everything a tad intense! Choose your path and jump right to racing!

The player, our sole user role, will control a vehicle – hover car in a futuristic city while being chased by an enemy or group of enemies. The goal is to reach a warp gate (which is always moving away from the player) as fast as possible. The player loses if they are caught by the enemy chasing them. Upon completion of the race, the player is shown their final time, which they may want to improve upon in future attempts.

There are four iterations in the project marked by milestons. In order, these are: "Initial Playable Scene, First Working Prototype, Play Testing/Asset Finalization, Final Product Due." More details are available in the link below that leads to Space Chase's Issues Tracking.

Each iteraction has its own range of issues and user stories. Such user stories are: "Test the Unity project's base functionality on an actual Samsung Gear VR headset, Implement player VR controls, Create Title Screen + Main Menu, and many more."

Game Controls

First Row: WASD Move the spaceship, Mouse to Accelarate or Propel the Spaceship Forward.
Second Row: Samsung Gear VR is used to look around game environment and tapping it will cause it to move forward and a Wireless XBox Controller is used to steer and fully control the ship.
